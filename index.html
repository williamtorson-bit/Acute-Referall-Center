ARC Guide

SLAM first checks

SLAM is indicated where the patient is under SLAM CMHT/PCMHT, lives in a SLAM borough and is not open elsewhere, is NFA presenting in a SLAM site and not open elsewhere, or is NFA detained under s136 in a SLAM borough.

London Compact

Provider responsibility

Adult admission is normally accepted by the Mental Health Trust for usual residence. If no residence is known, use the Trust closest to where assessed; for s136, use where first detained.

Who Pays?

Commissioner route

For ICB services, GP-associated ICB is normally responsible. If no GP, use usual residence. Funding uncertainty must not delay necessary assessment, care or treatment.

Trusted Assessment

Verbal decision

  • Why acute care and what admission achieves.
  • Reasonable alternatives explored.
  • Demographics and unique needs.
  • Holistic risk formulation and current risk management.
Escalation

Dispute pathway

Escalate bed manager/facility coordinator → on-call manager → on-call director → Surge/NHSE. Patient access should continue while responsibility is clarified.

Older adults

MHOA prompt

Consider PMOA/MHOA where primary dementia, older adult needs, frailty/physical illness complicating mental illness, or ageing-related needs are central.

Quick scripts
Short phrases for difficult phone calls. Edit before use.

Trusted Assessment challenge

“The assessment has covered the four Trusted Assessment elements: why acute care is required, alternatives explored, demographics/unique needs, and holistic risk management. On that basis, please confirm acceptance or identify the exact outstanding clinical question.”

Funding dispute

“Funding uncertainty should not delay necessary care. Please confirm the interim arrangement and we can resolve commissioner responsibility through the appropriate escalation route.”

No bed available

“The admission decision has been made. Please confirm actions taken to free capacity or source an alternative bed, and provide a timescale. If not resolved, we will escalate according to the Compact pathway.”
