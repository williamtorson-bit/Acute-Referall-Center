Knowledge base

Trusted Assessment verbal prompt

1. Why acute care?

What will admission or HTT achieve? Have reasonable community alternatives been explored and ruled out?

2. Demographics and unique needs

Communicate age, presentation site, legal status, safeguarding, gender/ward needs, disability, language, cultural, physical-health and neurodevelopmental needs.

3. Holistic risk formulation

Summarise biological, psychological and social risk formulation, current MSE, diagnosis/impression, dynamic risks and protective factors.

4. Risk management plan

Explain how risk will be managed while waiting, during conveyance and at handover, including risk to self, others and from others.

Escalation quick guide

Provider / bed responsibility dispute

Start bed manager/facility coordinator to bed manager. Escalate to on-call manager, then on-call director, then Surge / NHSE route where unresolved or breach risk is developing.

Do not delay care

Funding or commissioner uncertainty must not delay necessary assessment, treatment or admission. Use without-prejudice funding resolution where needed.

Operational triggers

HBPoS access delay over 4 hours: escalate to Surge. Inpatient admission delay over 6 hours: escalate. Delay over 12 hours: formal reporting / RCA process.
